This question bank for **CO3 — Enterprise Web Enabling & JS Frameworks** is compiled based on an analysis of the provided semester papers (June 2024, June 2025, Aug/Sept 2024, and Aug/Sept 2025) and the official syllabus.

### **Part A: Knowledge & Definitions (3 Marks)**

1. **Virtual DOM:** Define the Virtual DOM and list its pros in ensuring smooth rendering for real-time updates without reloading pages.
2. **JavaScript Versatility:** Highlight the suitability and value of using JavaScript across all layers of a full-stack enterprise application.
3. **Security Measures:** Explain the role of **CAPTCHA** in preventing unauthorized access by webbots during user registration for enterprise platforms.
4. **Single Page vs. Multi-Page:** Briefly mention the primary impact of **SPA versus MPA** on user experience and development efficiency.

### **Part B: Conceptual & Intermediate Analysis (10 Marks)**

1. **SPA vs. MPA Evaluation:** Evaluate the comparative advantages and trade-offs of Single-Page Applications (SPAs) versus Multi-Page Applications (MPAs). Discuss this in the context of an online marketplace (e.g., ShopSphere) focusing on **performance, scalability, and security**.
2. **Spring Ecosystem:** Assess the value and importance of the **SPRING and SPRING BOOT** frameworks for developing modular and efficient enterprise applications.
3. **MVC Architecture:** Explain how to apply **MVC Architecture** in enterprise development. List its specific benefits and provide an architectural sketch.
4. **JSP & Session Management:**
    - Evaluate the **server-side rendering mechanism** of Java Server Pages (JSP) and its advantages over alternative technologies.
    - Explain how to design **session management** for a shopping cart and payment module using JSP.

### **Part C: Advanced Problem Solving & Case Studies (12 Marks)**

1. **MERN vs. MEAN Stack Comparison:**
    - Compare and contrast the use of **MERN and MEAN stacks** for building a social networking site (e.g., Bookface or BookHive).
    - Discuss their architecture, strengths, and challenges regarding developer productivity and real-time interaction support.
2. **E-Learning Platform Design:**
    - Propose a JavaScript-based Full Stack architecture (MERN or MEAN) for an **e-learning platform**.
    - Discuss the implementation of **React components** (front-end), **Node.js/Express** (back-end), and **MongoDB** (data storage) to support real-time notifications for deadlines.
3. **Social Networking Requirements:** Formulate the requirements for a community platform where users browse authors, post comments, and receive real-time notifications. Justify your choice of a JS-based stack over a traditional Java stack.

---

### **Exam Strategy for CO3**

- **The "Guaranteed" Question:** Be prepared for a detailed comparison of **MERN vs. MEAN stacks** or **SPA vs. MPA**. One of these architectural trade-off questions appears in the Part B/C section of every paper.
- **The "Easy Wins" Topic:** Memorize the definition and benefits of the **Virtual DOM**. It is a frequent 3-mark question and a great way to explain React's performance in larger answers.
- **Case Study Application:** When discussing frameworks like **Spring Boot**, always link your answer to "enterprise modularity" and "rapid development" to align with examiner expectations.
- **Full Stack Justification:** Practice explaining why **JavaScript** is now preferred for the entire stack (front-end to back-end), citing benefits like "code reuse" and "handling concurrent real-time features".

### CO 3 UNIT 3 — Enterprise Web Enabling & JS Frameworks

**1. Most Important Topics**

- **SPA vs. MPA (Single Page vs. Multi-Page Applications)**
    - **Probability:** **98%**
    - **Toughness:** Easy
    - **Study Time Needed:** 1.5 Hours
    - **Type:** Concept-heavy
    - **Importance Reason:** Appears in every paper. Examiners focus on the comparative advantages regarding user experience (UX), development efficiency, performance, and scalability.
- **MERN vs. MEAN Stack (Architectural Comparison)**
    - **Probability:** **95%**
    - **Toughness:** Medium
    - **Study Time Needed:** 2.5 Hours
    - **Type:** Concept-heavy / Analytical
    - **Importance Reason:** This is the primary Part C topic for CO3. It is almost always tied to a case study like "Bookface" or "BookHive" (social networking) or e-learning platforms.
- **MVC Architecture, Spring, and Spring Boot**
    - **Probability:** **80%**
    - **Toughness:** Medium
    - **Study Time Needed:** 2 Hours
    - **Type:** Concept-heavy
    - **Importance Reason:** Frequently asked to assess the "value and importance" of these frameworks for enterprise modularity and development speed.
- **Virtual DOM (React)**
    - **Probability:** **75%**
    - **Toughness:** Easy
    - **Study Time Needed:** 1 Hour
    - **Type:** Concept-heavy
    - **Importance Reason:** A recurring Part A (3-mark) topic focusing on real-time rendering and performance pros.

**2. Most Probable Part C Questions (12 Marks)**

- **Social Network Case Study:** Compare and contrast the use of MERN and MEAN stacks for developing a site like "Bookface" or "BookHive." Discuss architecture, real-time notifications, and suitability for high-interaction environments.
- **E-Learning Platform Design:** Propose a JavaScript-based Full Stack architecture (MERN/MEAN) for an e-learning platform. Discuss the role of React/Angular components, Node.js/Express back-end, and MongoDB for real-time notifications and security (e.g., CAPTCHA).
- **SPA/MPA Evaluation:** Through a case study of an online marketplace (like ShopSphere), evaluate the trade-offs of SPA vs. MPA in terms of performance, security, and user experience.

**3. Most Probable Part B Questions (10 Marks)**

- **Framework Assessment:** Assess the value and importance of the SPRING and SPRING BOOT frameworks for enterprise application development, providing examples.
- **JavaScript Utility:** Justify the suitability and usefulness of JavaScript across all layers of a full-stack enterprise application, highlighting its benefits for scalability.
- **JSP Mechanism:** Evaluate the server-side rendering mechanism of Java Server Pages (JSP) and justify its advantages over alternative technologies regarding performance.
- **MVC Implementation:** How would you apply MVC architecture in enterprise development? List its specific benefits and sketch the architecture.

**4. Most Probable Part A Questions (3 Marks)**

- What is the Virtual DOM and what are its pros?
- List three characteristics of enterprise software with suitable examples. (Often appears as the lead-in to CO3/CO1 sections).
- Mention the role of CAPTCHA in user registration for enterprise sites.

**5. High Priority Topics**

- **MERN/MEAN Components:** You must be able to list and explain each letter (MongoDB, Express, React/Angular, Node) and their specific roles.
- **SPA/MPA Trade-offs:** Focus on the "No-reloading" benefit of SPAs vs. the SEO/initial load benefits of MPAs.

**6. Low Priority Topics**

- **Angular CLI details:** While in the syllabus, the papers focus more on high-level stack comparisons than specific CLI commands.
- **Hibernate/ORM:** Less frequent compared to Spring Boot and JS Frameworks in the analyzed papers.

### Final Strategy & Strategy Rankings

- **Ranked Topics by Probability:**
    1. SPA vs. MPA Comparison (98%)
    2. MERN vs. MEAN for Case Studies (95%)
    3. Spring Boot Value (80%)
    4. Virtual DOM Benefits (75%)

- **Top 20% Topics for 80% Marks:**
    - MERN vs. MEAN architecture diagram and component roles.
    - SPA vs. MPA trade-off table.
    - Spring Boot benefits for enterprise development.
- **Numerical/Code Probability:** **Low (5-10%)**. CO3 is almost entirely **concept-heavy and analytical**. You will likely be asked to "Sketch" an architecture or "Evaluate" a stack rather than write code.
