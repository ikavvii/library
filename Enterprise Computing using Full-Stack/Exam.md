### CO 1 UNIT 1 — Enterprise Foundations

**1. Most Important Topics**

- **Multi-tier/Full Stack Architecture Case Study**
    - Probability: **98%**
    - Toughness: Medium
    - Study Time Needed: 3 Hours
    - Type: Concept-heavy / Problem-solving-heavy
    - Importance Reason: Every single paper contains a major Part B or C question requiring the design of a multi-tier architecture for a specific scenario (University, Hospital, Bank, or Manufacturing).
- **Separation of Concerns (SoC)**
    - Probability: **85%**
    - Toughness: Easy
    - Study Time Needed: 1 Hour
    - Type: Concept-heavy
    - Importance Reason: Repeatedly linked with architecture design to justify modularity and maintenance.
- **Characteristics of Enterprise Software**
    - Probability: **70%**
    - Toughness: Easy
    - Study Time Needed: 0.5 Hours
    - Type: Memory-heavy
    - Importance Reason: Appears frequently in 3-mark Part A questions.

**2. Most Probable Part C Questions**

- Formulate requirements and design a Full Stack architecture (Java/JS) for a fragmented system (e.g., Hospital or University) that supports Separation of Concerns.
- Critical assessment of Java Full Stack vs. JS-based stacks for an e-commerce or purchase system.

**3. Most Probable Part B Questions**

- Propose a multi-tier architecture using established design principles for a Bank or Hospital case study.
- How do patterns and frameworks enhance or optimize enterprise applications?.

**4. High Priority Topics**

- **Architecture Case Studies:** Specifically Hospital and University scenarios as they have appeared multiple times.

**5. Low Priority Topics**

- Component-Based software development (Rarely questioned independently).

### CO 2 UNIT 2 — Enterprise Data Enabling

**1. Most Important Topics**

- **NoSQL in Enterprise Applications**
    - Probability: **95%**
    - Toughness: Easy
    - Study Time Needed: 1.5 Hours
    - Type: Concept-heavy
    - Importance Reason: Constant appearance comparing NoSQL to SQL or justifying its use in high-traffic/unstructured data scenarios.
- **Advanced JDBC (Connection Pooling & Distributed Transactions)**
    - Probability: **90%**
    - Toughness: Medium
    - Study Time Needed: 2 Hours
    - Type: Concept-heavy
    - Importance Reason: Frequently asked in Part B or C regarding performance and scalability.
- **SQL Injection & PreparedStatement**
    - Probability: **85%**
    - Toughness: Easy
    - Study Time Needed: 1 Hour
    - Type: Memory-heavy / Concept-heavy
    - Importance Reason: A staple for both Part A and Part B; often involves showing how `PreparedStatement` secures code.

**2. Most Probable Part C Questions**

- Discuss the role of SQL vs. NoSQL in data-enabling aspects of enterprise applications with case studies.
- Explain Connection Pooling and Distributed Transactions in JDBC and their practical applications in e-commerce.

**3. Most Probable Part B Questions**

- Compare and contrast JDBC application architecture with embedded SQL forms like SQLJ.
- Write a Java program/statements for basic CRUD operations or specific data retrieval (e.g., student marks > 60%).

**4. High Priority Topics**

- **Connection Pooling:** High marks-to-effort ratio.

**5. Low Priority Topics**

- Difference between Statement and ResultSet (Usually covered within larger JDBC questions).

### CO 4 UNIT 4 — Distributed Enterprise Communications

**1. Most Important Topics**

- **SOAP vs. RESTful Web Services**
    - Probability: **98%**
    - Toughness: Medium
    - Study Time Needed: 3 Hours
    - Type: Concept-heavy / Problem-solving-heavy
    - Importance Reason: The most consistent topic in Unit 4, covering comparisons, architectural constraints, and case study integration.
- **Web Services Design & Deployment (REST/SOAP)**
    - Probability: **85%**
    - Toughness: Hard
    - Study Time Needed: 4 Hours
    - Type: Memory-heavy / Concept-heavy
    - Importance Reason: Requires outlining step-by-step tasks, syntax, and semantics for creating/consuming services.
- **Microservices vs. APIs**
    - Probability: **75%**
    - Toughness: Easy
    - Study Time Needed: 1.5 Hours
    - Type: Concept-heavy
    - Importance Reason: Frequently asked to differentiate these from traditional web services or explain their role in simplification.

**2. Most Probable Part C Questions**

- Illustrate step-by-step tasks (Syntax/Semantics) for designing and consuming REST web services for a travel agency or online auction system.
- Design and deploy Java SOAP web services for secure data exchange (e.g., insurance policy verification).

**3. Most Probable Part B Questions**

- Analyze REST's architectural constraints (Statelessness, Cacheability, etc.) and their impact on scalability.
- Compare APIs, Web Services, and Microservices for inter-enterprise integration.

**4. High Priority Topics**

- **HTTP Methods in REST:** Vital for 3-mark and 6-mark questions.
- **SOAP Stack (WSDL, UDDI):** Necessary for theory questions on interoperability.

**5. Low Priority Topics**

- Synchronous vs. Asynchronous communication (Less frequent than SOAP/REST specific questions).

### Final Strategy & Rankings

- **Ranked Topics by Probability:**
    1. Full Stack Architecture Case Study (CO1) - 98%
    2. SOAP vs. REST Comparison (CO4) - 98%
    3. SPA vs. MPA Trade-offs (CO3) - 95%
    4. NoSQL vs. SQL Justification (CO2) - 95%
    5. MERN vs. MEAN for Social/E-comm (CO3) - 90%
- **Safest Scoring Topics:** SPA vs. MPA, NoSQL Benefits, SQL Injection prevention, Characteristics of Enterprise Software.
- **Hardest Topics:** Step-by-step REST/SOAP deployment syntax, writing JDBC code snippets.
- **Fastest Unit to Complete:** **Unit 1** (Concepts are repetitive and intuitive).
- **Highest Probability-to-Effort Ratio:** **Unit 3 (SPA/MPA)** and **Unit 2 (NoSQL)**. Minimal technical detail required for high marks.
- **Top 20% Topics to Score 80% Marks:**
    - Unit 1: Full Stack Architecture (Multi-tier design).
    - Unit 2: NoSQL, Connection Pooling, SQL Injection.
    - Unit 3: SPA/MPA, MERN vs. MEAN.
    - Unit 4: SOAP vs. REST comparison and REST architectural constraints.
- **Numerical/Code Probability:** **40%**. Expect at least one JDBC code-based question or a request for "syntax and semantics" in web services.
