This question bank for **CO1 — Enterprise Foundations** is compiled based on an analysis of the four provided semester papers (June 2024, June 2025, Aug/Sept 2024, and Aug/Sept 2025).

### **Part A: Knowledge & Definitions (3 Marks)**

1. **Define an Enterprise Application** and list any three benefits it provides to an organization.
2. **Identify three characteristics of enterprise software** and provide a suitable example for each.
3. **Explain Separation of Concerns (SoC)** and how it simplifies the development of Java and JavaScript-based full-stack applications.
4. **Describe the role of patterns and frameworks** in enhancing or optimizing enterprise-level applications.

### **Part B: Conceptual & Intermediate Analysis (10 Marks)**

1. **Full Stack Technology Assessment:** Perform a critical assessment of the **Java Full Stack versus a JavaScript-based Software Stack** (e.g., MEAN/MERN). Discuss the methodologies to be adopted for a specific case study, such as an **online purchase system** or a **multi-specialty hospital**.
2. **Framework Value:** Assess the value and importance of using established software stacks and development methodologies for **CityCare Hospitals** to integrate patient registration, doctor schedules, and billing into a unified system.
3. **Architectural Suggestion:** As an architect, suggest a full-stack architecture for a **university admission automation system**. Justify your choice of layers and technology stack while focusing on maintaining modularity and departmental autonomy.

### **Part C: Advanced Design & Case Studies (12-15 Marks)**

1. **University System Integration:** Formulate the requirements and challenges for integrating disparate university applications (Accounts, Academic Section, Exams) into a coherent whole. **Construct a multi-tier Full Stack architecture** that supports Separation of Concerns for this scenario.
2. **Hospital Enterprise Architecture:** Construct a Full Stack architecture for a hospital with fragmented patient data (registration, billing, pharmacy, etc.). Demonstrate how a **multi-tier architecture** can integrate these systems while ensuring flexibility for various client programs.
3. **Banking Centralization:** Propose a multi-tier/multi-layer architecture for a bank dealing with fragmented regional branches (savings, loans, credit cards). Justify your choice of **frameworks, database technologies, and middleware** to ensure secure and efficient service delivery.
4. **Manufacturing Sales Monitoring:** Sketch a **JavaScript-based architecture** that supports Separation of Concerns for a manufacturing firm (e.g., Supreme Products Pvt. Ltd.) to monitor regional sales, salesperson performance, and wholesaler orders.

---

### **Exam Strategy for CO1**

- **The "Guaranteed" Question:** Practice sketching **Multi-tier/Multi-layer architecture diagrams**. Every analyzed paper contains a 12-mark question requiring you to design an integrated architecture for a specific enterprise (University, Hospital, or Bank).
- **The "Easy Wins" Topic:** Memorize the **three characteristics of enterprise software** and the definition of **Separation of Concerns**. These are high-frequency, low-effort marks that regularly appear in Part A.
- **Design Precision:** When designing architectures, clearly label your layers (Presentation, Business Logic, Data Access) and specify the technology used in each (e.g., React for front-end, Node.js or Spring Boot for back-end).
- **Justification Skills:** For Part B, focus on _why_ one stack is chosen over another. Mention factors like **scalability, modularity, and developer productivity** to secure full marks.

### CO 1 UNIT 1 — Enterprise Foundations

**1. Most Important Topics**

- **Multi-tier / Multi-layer Full Stack Architecture**
    - Probability: **100%**
    - Toughness: Hard (due to design requirements)
    - Study Time Needed: 4 Hours
    - Type: Problem-solving-heavy
    - Importance Reason: Every single paper requires a Part C architecture design for a specific enterprise case study (University, Hospital, or Bank).
- **Separation of Concerns (SoC)**
    - Probability: **90%**
    - Toughness: Easy
    - Study Time Needed: 1 Hour
    - Type: Concept-heavy
    - Importance Reason: Frequently appears as a Part A question or as a mandatory design requirement within Part C case studies.
- **Full Stack Technology Assessment (Java vs. JavaScript/MEAN/MERN)**
    - Probability: **85%**
    - Toughness: Medium
    - Study Time Needed: 2 Hours
    - Type: Concept-heavy / Analytical
    - Importance Reason: The core of Part B questions, focusing on choosing the right stack for a scenario.

**2. Most Probable Part C Questions (12 Marks)**

- **University Integration:** Formulate requirements and construct a multi-tier Full Stack architecture that supports Separation of Concerns to integrate disparate university systems (Accounts, Registration, Exams).
- **Hospital Management:** Construct a multi-tier Full Stack architecture for a hospital (Patient Registration, Medical Records, Billing) to solve data fragmentation and ensure modularity.
- **Banking Systems:** Propose a centralized multi-tier architecture for a bank with fragmented regional branches (Savings, Loans, ATM) using established design principles to ensure efficient service delivery.
- **Manufacturing/Sales:** Sketch a JavaScript-based architecture that supports Separation of Concerns for a manufacturing firm monitoring sales across regional offices.

**3. Most Probable Part B Questions (10 Marks)**

- **Technology Assessment:** Perform a critical assessment of Java Full Stack versus a JavaScript stack (like MEAN) for an online purchase system or hospital management.
- **Framework Optimization:** Analyze how patterns and frameworks (like Spring Boot or React) enhance and optimize enterprise applications for modularity and departmental autonomy.
- **Case Study Requirements:** Analyze the challenges of disparate systems in an enterprise (like Core Banking) and propose how a Full Stack solution addresses them.

**4. Most Probable Part A Questions (3 Marks)**

- What is Separation of Concerns (SoC) and how does it simplify enterprise development?
- List three characteristics of enterprise software with suitable examples.
- Define an Enterprise application and list three benefits.
- How do patterns and frameworks optimize enterprise applications?

**5. High Priority Topics**

- **Case Studies:** Hospital, University, and Bank scenarios are the most frequent.
- **Architectural Diagrams:** Always practice sketching the layers (Presentation, Business/Logic, Data) for each case study.

**6. Low Priority Topics**

- Component-Based Software Development (Not featured as a major question in the last 4 papers, though in the syllabus).
