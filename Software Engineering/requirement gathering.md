### **Is it true that the success of a project relies heavily on a crucial yet often overlooked phase is Requirement Gathering? Justify the answer.**

**Answer:** **Yes, it is absolutely true.** Requirement gathering is the foundational phase of the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC), and the success of the entire project heavily depends on it.

**Justification:**

1. **Cost of Fixing Errors:** According to Boehm's cost-of-change curve, an error found during the requirement phase costs significantly less to fix than if it is discovered during testing or after deployment. Fixing a core requirement flaw post-deployment can cost up to 100 times more because it requires redesigning, recoding, and retesting.

2. **Aligns Stakeholder Expectations:** It acts as a bridge between the technical team and the business stakeholders. Clear requirements ensure that developers build what the client actually wants, not what the developers _think_ they want.

3. **Prevents Scope Creep:** Well-documented requirements define the exact boundaries of the system. Without this, new features constantly get added ad-hoc during development (scope creep), leading to missed deadlines and budget overruns.

4. **Foundation for Design and Testing:** System architecture, database design, and testing strategies (creating test cases) are all derived directly from the requirements. If the foundation is weak or ambiguous, the resulting software will be structurally flawed and difficult to validate.
