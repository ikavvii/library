> “A design solution difficult to understand would lead to increased development and maintenance cost”

This statement is a fundamental principle of software engineering. A design is only considered **simple and understandable if it is modular**.

- **Modularity & Complexity:** High complexity (spaghetti code) increases the **Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)** because developers must spend more time deciphering logic rather than building features.
- **The 100x Rule:** The cost of correcting a defect discovered in the design phase is the baseline (x). If the design is difficult to understand, errors often leak into production, where they cost **100 times more** to fix than in the design phase.
- **Technical Debt:** Poorly understood designs accumulate "interest" in the form of technical debt, making future modifications progressively more expensive and risky.
