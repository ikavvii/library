Based on the analysis of the provided examination papers, **CO3 (Object Oriented Analysis & Design)** is consistently featured as **Question 3** in the paper. The pattern is highly structured: it focuses on the relationship between design quality attributes (modularity, cohesion, coupling) and the practical application of UML diagrams for common real-world systems like ATMs and E-commerce.

### **Part A: Conceptual Foundations (3 Marks)**

_These questions typically ask for justifications or comments on design principles._

1. **Modularity and Simplicity:** "A design solution is simple and understandable, if it is modular." Comment on this statement.
2. **Maintenance Costs:** "A design solution difficult to understand would lead to increased maintenance cost." Comment on this statement.
3. **Cohesion vs. Coupling:** "Is it true that if there is an increase in the cohesion; coupling would automatically decrease? Justify the answer".

### **Part B: Intermediate Modeling & Design (7 Marks)**

_These questions focus on static modeling (Class diagrams) or specific logic-based dynamic models (State charts)._

1. **ATM Class Diagram:** Design a simple **Class Diagram** for ATM Banking software.
2. **State Transition Logic:** Draw a **State Chart** to depict a device with three radio buttons (b1, b2, and b3) where only one can be "on" at a time.
3. **Achieving Modularity:** "A design solution is simple and understandable, if it is modular." How is this modularity achieved?.

### **Part C: Advanced UML Modeling (15 Marks)**

_This section is almost exclusively problem-solving-heavy, requiring detailed diagrams for specific scenarios. Papers typically provide an "Either/Or" choice between an Activity Model or a combination of Sequence/Collaboration diagrams._

**Option 1: Activity Modeling (Workflow Logic)**

1. **ATM Cash Withdrawal:** Depict a complete **Activity Model** for withdrawing money from an ATM, including card validation, PIN retry logic (3 attempts), cash dispensing in multiples of 100, and insufficient fund handling.
2. **Mobile E-Commerce:** Design a very neat **Activity Model** to explain the steps in an **Online Shopping** website using a standard mobile app.
3. **Food Delivery App:** Design a neat **Activity Model** to explain the steps in **Ordering Food** using a standard mobile app.

**Option 2: Sequence & Collaboration Modeling (Interaction Logic)**

1. **Authenticated ATM Withdrawal:** Design neat **Sequence and Collaboration Diagrams** to explain the withdrawal of cash from an ATM, considering that the customer is already authenticated.
2. **Coffee Machine System:** Design a neat **Sequence Diagram** for a Coffee Machine interface. Include logic for coin slots (values of 5 and 10), price differences between tea (5) and coffee (10), change return, and cup delivery.

---

### **Strategic Preparation for CO3**

- **The "ATM" Dominance:** The ATM system appears in some form (Class, Activity, or Sequence diagram) in **100% of the papers analyzed**. Mastering every UML diagram for an ATM is the safest route to high marks.
- **High-Probability Theory:** Be prepared to explain why **High Cohesion** leads to **Low Coupling** and how modularity reduces maintenance costs. These are the only recurring theory topics in this unit.
- **Modeling Precision:** In Part C, examiners look for specific logic branches (e.g., what happens after 3 failed PIN attempts or how a machine handles exact change). Ensure your diagrams include these "alternative flows" to secure the full 15 marks.
- **Efficiency Tip:** Since Unit 3 is almost entirely diagram-based, practicing the **ATM withdrawal** and **Online Shopping** scenarios covers approximately 80% of the potential exam content.
