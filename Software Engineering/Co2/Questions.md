This question bank for **CO2 (Requirements Gathering)** is developed based on the consistent patterns identified in the four previous year examination papers. CO2 is always tested in **Question 2 (25 Marks)** and focuses heavily on the practical application of requirements classification, refinement, and use case modeling.

### **Part A: Fundamental Concepts (3 Marks)**

_These questions test the theoretical justification of requirements gathering as a critical phase._

1. **Role of Prototyping:** "Is it true that prototyping is a technique of building software which helps in uncovering and capturing software requirements from the client? Justify the answer.".
2. **Phase Significance:** "Is it true that the success of a project relies heavily on a crucial yet often overlooked phase: Requirement Gathering? Justify the answer.".

### **Part B: Classification & Refinement (7 Marks)**

_These questions require the ability to distinguish requirement types and fix poorly written specifications._

1. **Functional vs. Non-functional Classification:**
    - Identify and classify the functional and non-functional requirements for an **E-commerce website**.
    - Classify specific requirements for a **New Online Banking System** (e.g., email registration, encryption standards, authentication speed, and session timeouts).
    - Tabulate functional and non-functional requirements for a system admitting students to **Post Graduate Degrees** based on TANCET.
2. **Correcting Ambiguous Requirements:**
    - **Payroll System:** Highlight issues and rewrite: _"If an employee's salary exceeds a certain amount, the system should generate a notification to details to review the salary before processing the payroll"_.
    - **Academic Grading:** Correct the problems in: _"During the final grade computation, if any student scores a sufficiently low grade in a semester, then his/her parents would need to be informed"_.
    - **Exam System:** Rewrite for clarity: _"If a student fails to pay the exam fee on time, the system should notify the student before restricting access to the exam"_.
    - **Sales Reporting:** Fix the logic: _"If sales for current month are below target sales, then report is to be generated unless difference between target... is less than half of difference... in previous month"_.

### **Part C: SRS & Case Studies (15 Marks)**

_This section consistently demands the creation of UML Use Case models and detailed narratives for real-world scenarios._

1. **Gold Vending Machine (GVM):**
    - Design a neat **Use Case Model** depicting a GVM that accepts cards/cash, validates PINs, displays rates, and dispenses gold coins.
    - Write a complete **Use Case Story** to explain the logic of the GVM.
2. **Library Automation (OPAC):**
    - Design a **Use Case Model** for a college library’s Online Public Access Catalogue.
    - Write a **Use Case Story** specifically illustrating **Student Authentication** to access the library.
3. **Banking Software (Core Functions):**
    - Design a **Use Case Model** for Banking Software (specifically without ATM features).
    - Write a **Use Case Story** illustrating the procedure for the **Withdrawal of Cash**.

### **Strategic Preparation for CO2**

- **The "Must-Know" Trio:** You will almost certainly be asked to (1) Classify requirements, (2) Rewrite an ambiguous requirement, and (3) Draw a Use Case diagram with a story.
- **Safe Scoring:** Identifying **Functional vs. Non-functional** requirements is the easiest way to secure 7 marks; focus on system performance (speed, security) for non-functional and user actions for functional.
- **Most Predicted Scenario:** Given its appearance in three out of four papers, **Library Automation** or **Banking/Withdrawal** scenarios are the most likely topics for Part C.
