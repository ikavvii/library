### **CO 1 UNIT 1 — Software Engineering & Life Cycle Models**

**1. Most Important Topics**

- **COCOMO Model (Numerical)**
    - **Probability:** 100%
    - **Toughness:** Medium (Formula-heavy)
    - **Study Time Needed:** 3 Hours
    - **Type:** Problem-solving-heavy
    - **Importance Reason:** Appears as the primary Part C question in every single paper analyzed.
- **Agile vs. DevOps vs. Waterfall**
    - **Probability:** 90%
    - **Toughness:** Easy
    - **Study Time Needed:** 1.5 Hours
    - **Type:** Concept-heavy
    - **Importance Reason:** Frequent 7-mark question comparing methodologies or discussing the impact of going "Agile without DevOps".
- **Spiral vs. Prototyping for Risk**
    - **Probability:** 75%
    - **Toughness:** Easy
    - **Study Time Needed:** 1 Hour
    - **Type:** Concept-heavy
    - **Importance Reason:** Repeatedly used to test decision-making for high-risk projects.

**2. Most Probable Part C Questions**

- **Numerical estimation using COCOMO:** Calculate effort, duration, person-months, and total cost given KLOC and cost drivers (e.g., programmer capability, complexity).
- **Decomposition Techniques:** Estimating LOC for multiple modules to feed into COCOMO.

**3. Most Probable Part B Questions**

- Differences between Agile and DevOps or the specific benefits of DevOps.
- Recommendation of Prototyping vs. Spiral model for risky projects.

**4. High Priority Topics**

- **COCOMO Cost Drivers:** Understanding how factors like "Very Low" experience or "Very High" complexity affect the effort.

**5. Low Priority Topics**

- Detailed history of individual software models (focus instead on comparative analysis).

### **CO 2 UNIT 2 — Requirements Gathering**

**1. Most Important Topics**

- **Functional vs. Non-Functional Requirements**
    - **Probability:** 100%
    - **Toughness:** Easy
    - **Study Time Needed:** 1 Hour
    - **Type:** Concept-heavy / Memory-heavy
    - **Importance Reason:** Every paper asks students to classify requirements for a specific system like E-commerce or Banking.
- **Requirement Correction/Refinement**
    - **Probability:** 100%
    - **Toughness:** Medium
    - **Study Time Needed:** 2 Hours
    - **Type:** Problem-solving-heavy
    - **Importance Reason:** Students must identify ambiguity or issues in a poorly written requirement (e.g., "sufficiently low grade") and rewrite it.
- **Use Case Diagrams & Stories**
    - **Probability:** 100%
    - **Toughness:** Medium
    - **Study Time Needed:** 3 Hours
    - **Type:** Problem-solving-heavy
    - **Importance Reason:** Central Part C topic involving drawing diagrams and writing narratives for systems like Gold Vending Machines or Library OPAC.

**2. Most Probable Part C Questions**

- Design a **Use Case Model and Story** for a Banking system, Library Automation (OPAC), or Gold Vending Machine.

**3. Most Probable Part B Questions**

- Identify Functional/Non-functional requirements for an E-commerce site or Bank.
- Fixing "Ambiguous Requirements" provided in a snippet.

**4. High Priority Topics**

- **Prototyping in Requirements:** Its role in uncovering client needs (appears as a consistent 3-mark "Is it true?" question).

**5. Low Priority Topics**

- Formal IEEE SRS structure theory (papers favor practical classification and correction over listing the structure).

### **CO 3 UNIT 3 — Object Oriented Analysis & Design**

**1. Most Important Topics**

- **Dynamic Modeling (Activity & Sequence Diagrams)**
    - **Probability:** 100%
    - **Toughness:** Medium
    - **Study Time Needed:** 4 Hours
    - **Type:** Problem-solving-heavy
    - **Importance Reason:** These are the dominant Part C questions, usually focused on ATM withdrawals or Online Shopping.
- **Modularity, Cohesion, and Coupling**
    - **Probability:** 80%
    - **Toughness:** Medium
    - **Study Time Needed:** 2 Hours
    - **Type:** Concept-heavy
    - **Importance Reason:** Often appears in Part B discussing how simplicity and modularity reduce maintenance costs.
- **Class Diagrams**
    - **Probability:** 75%
    - **Toughness:** Easy
    - **Study Time Needed:** 1.5 Hours
    - **Type:** Problem-solving-heavy
    - **Importance Reason:** Standard 7-mark question for systems like ATM Banking.

**2. Most Probable Part C Questions**

- **Activity Model** for Online Shopping (ordering food/items) or ATM cash withdrawal.
- **Sequence/Collaboration Diagrams** for ATM withdrawal (authenticated scenario).

**3. Most Probable Part B Questions**

- Design a **Class Diagram** for ATM software.
- Explaining the relationship between **Cohesion and Coupling**.

**4. High Priority Topics**

- **State Chart Diagrams:** Logic involving radio buttons or simple state transitions.

**5. Low Priority Topics**

- UI Design principles (theory) — the papers are almost entirely focused on UML diagram generation.

### **CO 4 UNIT 4 — Software Testing Fundamentals**

**1. Most Important Topics**

- **Basis Path Testing (Cyclomatic Complexity)**
    - **Probability:** 100%
    - **Toughness:** Hard
    - **Study Time Needed:** 4 Hours
    - **Type:** Problem-solving-heavy
    - **Importance Reason:** Guaranteed 15-mark question involving writing pseudo-code, calculating complexity, and generating test cases.
- **Boundary Value Analysis (BVA) / Robustness Testing**
    - **Probability:** 75%
    - **Toughness:** Medium
    - **Study Time Needed:** 2 Hours
    - **Type:** Problem-solving-heavy
    - **Importance Reason:** Often appears as an alternative or supplementary Part C/B question for date validation or range checks.
- **Testing Principles (Defect Clustering, Pareto, etc.)**
    - **Probability:** 80%
    - **Toughness:** Easy
    - **Study Time Needed:** 1 Hour
    - **Type:** Memory-heavy
    - **Importance Reason:** Standard 3-7 mark questions exploring "Exhaustive testing is impossible" or the "Pareto Principle".

**2. Most Probable Part C Questions**

- **Basis Path Testing numerical:** Given a snippet of code (Arithmetic operations, Positive/Negative count), draw a flow graph and find the Cyclomatic Complexity.
- **BVA and Robustness testing** for a "Next Date" program or ASCII/Alphabet validation.

**3. Most Probable Part B Questions**

- **Black Box testing test case design** for functions like "Quadratic Solver" or "Customer Authentication".
- Comments on "Exhaustive Testing is Impossible" or the sequence of testing (Black box then White box).

**4. High Priority Topics**

- **Path Testing logic:** Implementing pseudo-code for interest rate calculation or simple loops.

**5. Low Priority Topics**

- Web and GUI testing specific tools (focus on the _methodology_ of BVA/Path testing instead).

### **Strategic Exam Summary**

- **Ranked Topics by Probability:**
    1. **COCOMO Numerical** (100%)
    2. **Basis Path Testing / Cyclomatic Complexity** (100%)
    3. **Use Case Model & Narrative** (100%)
    4. **Activity/Sequence Diagrams (ATM Context)** (100%)
    5. **Functional/Non-Functional Classification** (100%)
- **Safest Scoring Topics:** Classification of requirements and Agile/DevOps theory.
- **Hardest Topics:** Basis Path Testing (requires accurate flow graph construction) and COCOMO (requires careful calculation with cost drivers).
- **Fastest Units to Complete:** **Unit 2** (Requirements) — Very intuitive and repetitive.
- **Highest Probability-to-Effort Ratio Topics:**
    - Functional vs. Non-functional (1 Hour = 7 Marks).
    - Prototyping vs. Spiral for Risk (1 Hour = 7 Marks).
- **Top 20% Topics for 80% Marks:**
    - COCOMO Numerical.
    - Requirement Correction.
    - Use Case/Activity/Sequence Diagrams.
    - Basis Path Testing.
- **Numerical Probability:** The probability of a numerical/diagram-based problem appearing in Part C for any given unit is **100%**. There are virtually no pure "theory" essay questions in Part C.
