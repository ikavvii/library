Highlight the purpose and the process followed in the Turing Test.
### **Purpose of the Turing Test**

The primary purpose of the Turing Test is to provide a functional definition of intelligence. It seeks to determine whether a machine can **demonstrate human-like intelligence** to the extent that its behavior is indistinguishable from that of a human. Instead of debating the philosophical question "Can machines think?", the test focuses on whether a machine can **imitate** human cognitive responses.

### **The Process Followed**

The test traditionally involves a specific "imitation game" setup:

- **The Participants:** There are three participants: a **human interrogator**, a **human subject**, and the **machine** being tested.
- **The Setting:** The interrogator is placed in a separate room from the other two. To ensure the test evaluates intelligence rather than physical appearance or voice, communication is conducted through a **text-based interface** (like a keyboard and screen).
- **The Interaction:** The interrogator asks a series of questions to both the human and the machine. The machine’s goal is to trick the interrogator into believing it is the human, while the human's goal is to help the interrogator identify the machine.
- **The Outcome:** If the interrogator cannot reliably tell which participant is the human and which is the machine after a fixed period of questioning, the machine is said to have **passed the Turing Test** and is considered "intelligent."
