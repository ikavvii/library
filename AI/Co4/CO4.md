Here is the structured question bank for **CO4 — Uncertain Knowledge, Probability Theory, and Learning Strategies**. This outcome focuses on handling uncertainty and the development of intelligent learning systems.

### **Part A: Probability Fundamentals & Concepts (3–5 Marks)**

1. **Sample Space Derivation:** Explain how a sample space is derived and represent the sample space for the following experiment: "Mani could learn a scientific or historical course in class or at home".
2. **Mutually Exclusive Events:**
    - Define when events are said to be mutually exclusive.
    - Solve: If $A$ and $B$ are two mutually exclusive events of an experiment, and $P(A')=0.65$ and $P(A \cup B)=0.65$, find $P(B)$.
3. **Independence in Probability:** Consider the experiment of rolling a pair of dice. Event ‘P’ is a sum that is even; Event ‘Q’ is that both numbers are greater than 4. Determine if these two events are independent and justify your answer using probabilities.
4. **Baye’s Rule Definition:** State Baye's rule and clearly mention and define the terms involved in its mathematical representation.

### **Part B: Learning Strategies (7–15 Marks)**

1. **Comparison of Approaches:** Compare and contrast the **supervised and unsupervised** approaches of learning in Artificial Intelligence, providing apt examples for each.
2. **General Learning Frameworks:**
    - Machine learning focuses on programs that access data for their own learning. Describe various methods/approaches of learning in AI with examples and their applications.
3. **Probability Calculation (Set Theory):** If $A$ and $B$ are two events such that $P(A)=1/4, P(B)=1/2$, and $P(A \cap B)=1/8$, find the probability of **"not A and not B"**.

### **Part C: Bayesian Networks & Advanced Inference (10–15 Marks)**

1. **Bayesian Network Components:** Analyze the importance and roles of the **Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG)** and **Conditional Probability Table (CPT)** components within a Bayesian network.
2. **Network Analysis and Calculation:** Explain the structure of a Bayesian Network. Analyze a provided network (e.g., involving Cloudy weather, Rains, and Wet Grass) and use the associated CPTs to calculate specific probabilities, such as the probability of having wet grass.
3. **Baye's Rule Application (Diagnosis/Industry):** These high-value questions require applying the rule to real-world scenarios:
    - **Case 1 (Late Delivery):** Company A supplies 40% of computers (5% late), Company B supplies 30% (3% late), and Company C supplies 30% (2.5% late). If a computer arrives late, calculate the probability it was supplied by Company A.
    - **Case 2 (Medical Diagnosis):** In a neighborhood, 90% of children fall sick due to flu and 10% due to measles. The probability of observing rashes is 0.95 for measles and 0.08 for flu. If a child develops rashes, calculate the probability that the child has measles.

---

### **Exam Intelligence for CO4**

- **Calculations are Crucial:** Unlike CO1 and CO2, which are often diagram-heavy, CO4 relies heavily on numerical accuracy. Be prepared to show every step of your probability calculations.
- **Baye’s Rule Dominance:** One of the two choices in the 15-mark "Part C" section is almost guaranteed to be a multi-variable Baye’s Rule problem involving three different sources or two different medical conditions.
- **The Learning Comparison:** "Supervised vs. Unsupervised learning" is a consistent 7-mark or 15-mark theoretical question. Ensure you have distinct examples for each (e.g., classification for supervised, clustering for unsupervised).
- **Bayesian Network Terminology:** You must be able to define the components (DAG/CPT) and also perform the "chaining" of probabilities across the nodes of the graph.
