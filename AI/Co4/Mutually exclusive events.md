According to probability theory, events are said to be mutually exclusive if they **cannot occur at the same time**.

The following properties apply to mutually exclusive events:

- **Mathematical Condition:** If two events $A$ and $B$ are mutually exclusive, the probability of both occurring simultaneously is zero: **$P(A \cap B) = 0$**.
- **The Addition Rule:** For mutually exclusive events, the probability that either $A$ or $B$ occurs is simply the sum of their individual probabilities: **$P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B)$**,.
