Modus Ponens: from p and p → q, derive q 
Modus Tollens: from ¬q and p → q, derive ¬p 
Hypothetical Syllogism: from p → q and q → r, derive p → r 
Disjunctive Syllogism: from p ∨ q and ¬p, derive q 
Addition: from p, derive p ∨ q 
Simplification: from p ∧ q, derive p 
Conjunction: from p and q, derive p ∧ q 
Resolution: from p ∨ q and ¬p ∨ r, derive q ∨ r

Rules of inference are ==logical argument forms used to deduce valid conclusions from a set of premises==. In formal logic, they guarantee that if the premises are true, the conclusion must logically be true as well.
